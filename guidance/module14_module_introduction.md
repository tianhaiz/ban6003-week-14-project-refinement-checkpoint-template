# Module 14 Introduction: Interpretation, Ethics, Recommendations, and Project Refinement

The project is not finished when the model runs. A model output by itself is not enough. This module helps you turn technical work into a responsible analytics story: what the result means, what it does not mean, what limitations remain, and how the result should be used.

This week also functions as a final project refinement workshop. You will revise both sides of the project: the data management story and the analytics story.

## This Week You Will

- interpret regression, classification, or other analytical results in business language;
- explain data and model limitations;
- discuss ethics, bias, fairness, and responsible use;
- connect findings to cautious business recommendations;
- revise your report structure and presentation storyline;
- identify gaps before final submission.

## Lab Focus

The lab is not a heavy coding lab. It gives you a structured notebook for drafting or revising your executive summary, business problem, data sources, profiling summary, cleaning and transformation summary, integration and ABT summary, data dictionary explanation, model interpretation, limitations, ethics, recommendations, and presentation storyline.

## Communication Standard

Do not simply paste metrics or model output. Explain what the result means for the business question. Use cautious language such as "the model suggests," "the results are consistent with," and "this model can support this decision, but it cannot prove..."

## Project Connection

Use this week seriously as a project workshop. If your ABT unit of analysis, limitations, ethics section, or recommendation logic is unclear, now is the time to revise.
